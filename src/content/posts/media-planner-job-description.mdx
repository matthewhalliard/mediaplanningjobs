A media planner job description needs to work for two audiences: employers hiring for the role and candidates researching whether it's the right fit. Most JD templates online are either too vague ("develop media strategies") or hopelessly outdated (no mention of retail media networks, CTV, or programmatic). This guide fixes that.

Below you'll find a copy-paste job description template, a breakdown of core media planner responsibilities, the skills that matter in 2026, and what to include in your requirements section.

## What does a media planner actually do?

Media planners determine where, when, and how advertising budgets get spent. You're the strategist who decides a CPG brand should allocate 35% to CTV, 25% to social, 20% to programmatic display, and 20% to retail media—not the buyer executing those placements (though at smaller agencies, you'll do both).

The role breaks into five core functions:

**Audience and market research.** You analyze first-party data, third-party audience segments, competitive spend, and media consumption patterns to identify where your target customer actually spends time. In 2026, that means understanding retail media networks (Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect, Kroger Precision Marketing) as much as traditional publisher inventory.

**Media strategy development.** You build the plan: channels, budget allocation, flighting, dayparting, creative formats. Post-cookie depreciation (which finally landed Q1 2026), this increasingly means contextual targeting strategies and first-party data activation rather than third-party cookie pools.

**Budget management and forecasting.** You model CPMs, CPCs, view-through rates, and incrementality across channels to forecast performance and justify budget splits. Excel or Google Sheets fluency isn't optional—you're building models that scenario-plan $500K quarterly budgets.

**Vendor and publisher negotiations.** You negotiate rates, value-adds, and make-goods. Even if a dedicated [media buyer](/jobs/function/media-buyer/) handles execution, you set the parameters and approve the deal terms.

**Performance tracking and optimization.** You monitor pacing, attribution, and incrementality throughout the flight, then recommend mid-campaign shifts. AI-assisted planning tools now surface optimization opportunities faster, but you still make the call on whether to reallocate budget from underperforming placements.

## Media planner responsibilities: the full list

When writing a media planner job description, these responsibilities should appear in priority order based on your agency or brand's focus:

- Develop comprehensive media plans aligned to campaign objectives, target audiences, and budget parameters
- Conduct audience research using first-party data, syndicated tools (MRI-Simmons, Comscore), and platform insights
- Build budget allocation models across paid search, paid social, programmatic display, CTV, audio, and retail media
- Analyze competitive media spend and share-of-voice in category
- Recommend channel mix, flighting strategies, and creative formats based on reach and frequency goals
- Collaborate with creative, strategy, and analytics teams to align media tactics with campaign messaging
- Negotiate CPMs, sponsorships, and added-value opportunities with publishers and platform reps
- Monitor campaign pacing, delivery, and performance against KPIs (reach, frequency, CPM, CTR, conversions, ROAS)
- Identify optimization opportunities mid-flight and reallocate budgets to higher-performing tactics
- Prepare pre-campaign proposals, flowcharts, and post-campaign performance recaps for clients
- Maintain relationships with media vendors, platform reps, and publisher sales teams
- Stay current on platform updates, ad format launches, and industry trends (privacy regulation, measurement standards, emerging channels)

For senior media planner roles, add:

- Mentor junior planners and assistant planners on strategy development and client communication
- Lead new business pitches and RFP responses
- Manage client relationships and present strategic recommendations in quarterly business reviews

## Required skills for media planners in 2026

**Platform fluency.** You need working knowledge of the platforms you're planning into. That means understanding Trade Desk, Google DV360, Meta Ads Manager, Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect, Roku, and YouTube's buying interfaces—even if a [programmatic specialist](/jobs/function/programmatic/) or buyer is doing the hands-on-keyboard execution. You should know what's targetable, what CPMs look like, and what the attribution window is.

**Analytical modeling.** Media planning is a spreadsheet job. You're building reach/frequency curves, CPM comparisons, budget allocation scenarios, and incrementality models. If you're not comfortable with Excel pivot tables, VLOOKUP, and basic statistical concepts, you'll struggle.

**First-party data strategy.** Post-cookie, planning revolves around activating client first-party data (CRM files, website visitors, loyalty program members) and contextual targeting. You need to understand data onboarding, match rates, and how to build audiences in clean rooms (Google Ads Data Hub, Amazon Marketing Cloud, LiveRamp).

**Retail media network knowledge.** Retail media is now 15-20% of most CPG media plans. You need to understand how Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect, Roundel (Target), and Kroger Precision Marketing differ in audience scale, targeting capabilities, and attribution.

**CTV and streaming fluency.** CTV programmatic is consolidating but still fragmented. You should know the difference between buying directly in Roku or Hulu vs. using a DSP to access their inventory, and when each makes sense.

**Negotiation and relationship management.** You're negotiating with Google reps, publisher sales teams, and podcast networks. Being able to push back on a CPM or ask for bonus impressions matters.

**Communication and presentation.** You're presenting plans to clients, writing rationale documents, and defending budget recommendations in meetings. Clear, concise writing and confident presenting aren't soft skills—they're core to the job.

**AI-assisted planning tools.** Tools like Omnicom's Omni, Publicis' Marcel, and third-party platforms are automating media mix modeling, budget optimization, and forecasting. You don't need to be a data scientist, but you need to interpret AI-generated recommendations and know when to override them.

## Media planner job description template (copy-paste ready)

Use this template as a starting point. Customize the channel mix, tools, and experience level to match your needs.

---

**Job Title:** Media Planner  
**Department:** Media Planning  
**Reports to:** Media Planning Supervisor / Associate Media Director  
**Location:** [City, State / Remote / Hybrid]

**About the role**

We're looking for a media planner to develop and execute data-driven media strategies for [agency clients / our brand]. You'll own audience research, budget allocation modeling, channel recommendations, and performance tracking across paid search, paid social, programmatic, CTV, and retail media networks. This role partners closely with media buyers, analytics, and strategy teams to deliver measurable campaign results.

**Responsibilities**

- Build comprehensive media plans that align channel tactics, audience targeting, and budget allocation to campaign objectives
- Conduct audience and competitive research using first-party data, MRI-Simmons, Comscore, and platform insights
- Develop budget allocation models and reach/frequency forecasts across digital and traditional channels
- Recommend media mix, flighting strategies, geo-targeting, and creative formats based on performance goals
- Collaborate with buyers to negotiate CPMs, sponsorships, and value-adds with publishers and platform reps
- Monitor campaign pacing, delivery, and KPIs; identify optimization opportunities and recommend budget reallocations
- Prepare pre-campaign flowcharts, budget breakdowns, and post-campaign performance recaps
- Stay current on platform updates (Trade Desk, DV360, Meta, Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect), privacy regulation, and industry measurement standards
- Mentor assistant planners on research methodologies and planning best practices [senior roles only]

**Required qualifications**

- Bachelor's degree in marketing, advertising, communications, or related field
- [2-4 years] experience in media planning at an agency or brand
- Fluency in digital media platforms: Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager, Trade Desk or DV360, Amazon DSP
- Advanced Excel/Google Sheets skills: pivot tables, VLOOKUP, budget modeling
- Experience with analytics platforms (Google Analytics 4, Adobe Analytics) and data visualization tools
- Understanding of attribution models, incrementality testing, and marketing mix modeling
- Strong written and verbal communication skills; comfortable presenting to clients

**Preferred qualifications**

- Experience planning retail media campaigns (Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect, Kroger Precision Marketing, Roundel)
- CTV and audio planning experience across Roku, Hulu, YouTube, Spotify
- Certifications: Trade Desk Edge Academy, Google Ads, Meta Blueprint
- Familiarity with first-party data activation, clean rooms, and contextual targeting strategies
- Experience with media planning tools (Prisma, Strata, Mediaocean) or AI-assisted planning platforms

**Compensation**

[Salary range based on experience: $65K-$85K for mid-level roles, $85K-$115K for senior roles at agencies in major markets]

## How to customize this template for your organization

**For agency roles:** Emphasize client management, presentation skills, and multi-client juggling. List the specific agency tools you use (Prisma, Strata, Mediaocean). If you specialize in a vertical (healthcare, CPG, finance), call that out and adjust the platform list accordingly.

**For in-house brand roles:** Focus on stakeholder collaboration, long-term strategy, and first-party data expertise. Brand-side planners typically go deeper on measurement, incrementality, and marketing mix modeling than agency counterparts. Mention your attribution platform (Neustar, Nielsen, Google Analytics 4) and any proprietary audience segments.

**For entry-level roles:** Drop the years-of-experience requirement and emphasize internships, coursework, or certifications. Highlight mentorship and training programs. Be realistic about what a coordinator or assistant planner will actually do (a lot of spreadsheet building, deck formatting, and campaign tracking before they own full strategy).

**For senior roles:** Add people management, new business development, and client relationship ownership. Senior planners at agencies often lead pitches, present in QBRs, and train junior staff. If the role includes budget authority or P&L responsibility, state it clearly.

## Where media planning fits in the broader media team

Media planning sits between [strategy](/jobs/function/media-strategy/) and execution. Strategists set the brand positioning and campaign objectives. Planners translate that into a channel plan and budget model. [Buyers](/jobs/function/media-buyer/) execute the placements. [Ad ops](/jobs/function/ad-operations/) traffic the tags. [Analysts](/jobs/function/media-analyst/) measure the results. In smaller orgs, one person wears multiple hats.

If you're hiring and not sure whether you need a planner, a buyer, or a hybrid role, here's the test: if you need someone to decide *where* to spend, hire a planner. If you need someone to negotiate *how much* and manage vendor relationships, hire a buyer. If your budget is under $500K annually, hire one person who can do both.

## What to look for when hiring a media planner

Portfolios matter more than resumes. Ask candidates to walk you through a media plan they built: what was the objective, how did they allocate budget, what were the results, and what would they do differently? Good planners can articulate trade-offs (why they chose CTV over linear, or Meta over TikTok) with data.

Test their Excel skills in the interview. Give them a budget allocation scenario and watch them build the model in real time. If they can't pivot a table or build a quick formula, they'll struggle in the role.

Ask about a time they had to optimize a failing campaign mid-flight. The answer reveals how they troubleshoot, whether they rely on data or gut, and how they communicate bad news to clients.

Check their platform knowledge without making it a pop quiz. "Walk me through how you'd set up a prospecting campaign in Trade Desk" or "What's your process for building a custom audience in Meta?" separates candidates who've actually done the work from those who list every platform on their resume.

For senior hires, ask them to present a mock QBR or pitch. You're hiring someone who'll be in front of clients or executives regularly—see how they perform under pressure.

## FAQs

**What are the core responsibilities of a media planner?**

Media planners develop media strategies, conduct audience research, allocate budgets across channels, negotiate rates, create media plans, and track campaign performance. In 2026, this includes managing programmatic buys, CTV placements, and retail media networks alongside traditional channels.

**What skills does a media planner need in 2026?**

Modern media planners need fluency in DSPs (Trade Desk, DV360), analytics platforms (GA4, Adobe Analytics), Excel/Google Sheets modeling, retail media networks (Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect), and AI-assisted planning tools. Strong analytical, negotiation, and communication skills remain critical.

**What's the difference between a media planner and media buyer?**

Media planners develop the strategy—determining which channels, audiences, timing, and budget allocation will achieve campaign goals. Media buyers execute that strategy by negotiating rates, placing orders, and managing vendor relationships. At smaller agencies, one person often does both.

**What qualifications do you need to become a media planner?**

Most media planner roles require a bachelor's degree in marketing, advertising, communications, or related fields. Entry-level positions value internships at agencies or brands, foundational digital marketing knowledge, and Excel proficiency. Certifications in Google Ads, Trade Desk, or Meta Blueprint help but aren't mandatory.

**What's the typical salary range for media planners in 2026?**

Entry-level media planners at agencies earn $50K-$65K. Mid-level planners with 3-5 years earn $65K-$85K. Senior planners command $85K-$115K. In-house brand roles typically pay 10-20% higher. Major markets (NYC, LA, SF, Chicago) add another 10-15% premium.
