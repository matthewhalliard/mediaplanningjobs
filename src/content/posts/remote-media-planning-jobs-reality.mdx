Most agency media planning jobs in 2026 aren't remote—they're hybrid, with three days in-office as the standard compromise. But if you know where to look, real remote opportunities exist in programmatic, ad ops, and in-house brand roles. Here's the honest breakdown of what's actually available and which employers are hiring remotely.

The holdcos (GroupM, Publicis, Omnicom, IPG, Dentsu) settled into 3-day hybrid by mid-2024 and haven't budged. Planning, especially at full-service agencies, still leans on in-person collaboration—client meetings, cross-functional briefs, whiteboard sessions. If you're searching for [media planner](/jobs/function/media-planner/) roles at traditional agencies expecting full remote, you'll be disappointed.

The remote jobs that do exist cluster in three areas: programmatic trading and campaign management, ad ops and trafficking, and in-house media roles at digital-native brands. Each has different expectations, different employers, and different compensation.

## Programmatic and ad ops: the remote-friendly tier

Programmatic roles—campaign managers, traders, analysts—offer the most consistent remote options. The work is platform-native (Trade Desk, DV360, Amazon DSP), performance-driven, and less dependent on physical presence. Agencies like Tinuiti, Wpromote, and Goodway Group hire remote [programmatic](/jobs/function/programmatic/) specialists across the U.S., particularly for client accounts that don't require regular in-person check-ins.

At Tinuiti, mid-level programmatic campaign managers earn $75K-$95K and work fully remote in many cases. Senior programmatic traders managing retail media or CTV buys can push $100K-$125K, especially if they specialize in Amazon DSP or Walmart Connect—two platforms still expanding headcount in 2026.

Ad ops jobs are similarly remote-viable. Trafficking, tagging, creative QA, and reporting don't require office presence. Agencies like Horizon Media and Havas have remote [ad ops](/jobs/function/ad-operations/) roles, though titles vary (Ad Operations Specialist, Campaign Trafficker, Digital Operations Manager). Salaries for mid-level ad ops specialists run $65K-$85K; senior managers with programmatic or CTV experience hit $90K-$115K.

The platforms themselves—Trade Desk, Roku, Viant, StackAdapt—offer remote campaign management and client success roles. Trade Desk's campaign managers (supporting agency clients) are often remote and earn $85K-$110K base. Roku's ad ops team, supporting CTV inventory and direct-sold campaigns, has remote positions in the $95K-$125K range for senior ICs.

## In-house brand roles: D2C and tech favor remote

D2C brands and tech companies with mature media teams offer the most flexibility for traditional media planning work done remotely. Brands like Warby Parker, Casper, Hims & Hers, and ThredUp have in-house media planners who work remotely or on flexible hybrid schedules (1-2 days in-office, not three).

These roles skew toward paid social, paid search, and programmatic display rather than traditional linear TV or out-of-home. If you're a planner with Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, and Trade Desk experience, you're more hirable remotely than someone whose book is 60% broadcast TV.

Mid-level media planners at D2C brands earn $85K-$110K; senior planners or media managers run $110K-$140K. Titles vary—Media Manager, Growth Marketing Manager, Performance Media Lead—but the work is media planning and buying, often managing a mix of agency partners and direct platform buys.

Tech platforms expanding ad businesses (Reddit, Pinterest, Snap, TikTok) also hire remote media strategists and account managers to support agency and brand partners. These aren't traditional planning roles, but they require planning skills: understanding campaign objectives, recommending targeting and creative strategies, analyzing performance. Compensation ranges from $90K-$120K for mid-level to $120K-$160K for senior client-facing roles.

## What "remote" actually means in 2026

Remote doesn't always mean work-from-anywhere. Many employers specify "remote within the U.S." or require residence in specific states (often excluding California, New York, or Washington due to tax and labor law complexity). Some require proximity to a major city for quarterly in-person meetings or client visits.

And "remote" at agencies often means "remote until the client wants you on-site." If you're managing a retail account and the CMO wants quarterly business reviews in Bentonville, you're traveling. If you're on a CPG brand and the client holds planning sessions in Cincinnati, same deal. Budget for 4-8 trips per year even in nominally remote roles.

The few fully distributed, no-office agencies (like Barbarian, which went remote-first in 2021, or smaller performance shops) offer true location flexibility. But they're rare and competitive. Expect rigorous interview processes and a portfolio that proves you can manage ambiguity and deliver without supervision.

## Where traditional planning stays hybrid

Full-service agency planning—the kind that spans linear TV, OOH, digital, social, and integrated campaigns—remains stubbornly hybrid. GroupM agencies (Mindshare, Wavemaker, EssenceMediacom) require planners in-office three days. Publicis (Starcom, Zenith, Spark Foundry) same. Omnicom (OMD, PHD, Hearts & Science) same.

Dentsu has select remote roles, mostly in programmatic and analytics, but traditional planning is hybrid. IPG (UM, Initiative, Mediahub) is the most flexible of the holdcos, with some fully remote senior planning roles if you're on accounts that don't demand in-person collaboration, but that's account-specific, not policy.

Independent full-service shops (Horizon, Connelly Partners, Mistress) are split. Some went remote-first during COVID and stayed that way; others reverted to hybrid or full in-office by 2025. Check the job posting carefully—"remote" in the title doesn't always mean what you think.

## The junior planner problem

If you're early-career, remote options shrink further. Agencies want junior [media planners](/jobs/function/media-planner/) in the office to learn by osmosis—sitting in on client calls, shadowing senior planners, absorbing how media plans get built and sold. Most assistant planner and associate planner roles require four or five days on-site, not three.

The exceptions: ad ops and programmatic junior roles at tech platforms or performance agencies. Trade Desk, Amazon Advertising, and Criteo hire remote associate campaign managers and train them via structured onboarding programs. Tinuiti and Wpromote have remote junior programmatic roles with mentorship built in. Salaries are lower—$50K-$65K for entry-level—but the roles exist.

If you're trying to break into planning remotely with no experience, your path is narrow: target ad ops or programmatic specialist roles at platforms or performance shops, get 12-18 months of experience, then lateral into broader planning.

## Who's actually hiring remote in 2026

As of mid-2026, these employers have active or frequent remote media planning, programmatic, and ad ops roles:

**Performance agencies:** Tinuiti, Wpromote, Goodway Group, PMG, Media.Monks (programmatic arm).

**Platforms:** Trade Desk (campaign management, account management), Amazon Advertising (account executives, ad ops), Walmart Connect (campaign managers), Roku (ad ops, sales planning), Reddit (agency partnerships), Pinterest (media partnerships).

**D2C brands:** Warby Parker, Hims & Hers, Bombas, Allbirds, ThredUp, Vuori, Italic (titles vary: Media Manager, Growth Marketing Manager, Performance Lead).

**Holdco outliers:** Dentsu (select programmatic roles), IPG Mediahub (senior planners on specific accounts), Publicis Epsilon (data-driven media roles).

**Independent agencies:** Barbarian, Power Digital, Silverback Strategies, 3Q Digital (mostly paid search and paid social, but media planning-adjacent).

Check [remote media planning jobs](/jobs/) on niche boards, not just LinkedIn. Many smaller agencies and brands post on We Work Remotely, FlexJobs, or directly on their careers pages without broadcasting widely.

## The trade-offs of remote planning work

Remote planning jobs often mean narrower scope. You're less likely to work on integrated campaigns spanning TV, OOH, digital, and experiential. You're more likely to own a channel (programmatic display, paid social, Amazon DSP) or a performance KPI (CPA, ROAS) rather than brand-building work.

Compensation is competitive but not always better than hybrid roles. A remote programmatic manager at Tinuiti earns $85K-$100K; a hybrid planner at Mindshare in New York might make $90K-$105K. The difference is cost of living and career path, not a remote premium.

Career progression can be slower. Agencies still promote based on client relationships and visibility, which are harder to build remotely. If you want to make media director by 30, a hybrid role at a holdco might get you there faster than a remote role at a performance shop.

But for experienced planners who value flexibility, hate commutes, or live outside major markets, remote work is viable in 2026. You just have to know which doors are actually open.
