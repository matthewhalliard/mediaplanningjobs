If you're building a [programmatic](/jobs/function/programmatic/) career in 2026, the DSP you learn first determines which doors open. The Trade Desk is the safest bet for breadth across agencies and brands. DV360 locks you into Google's ecosystem but pays off if that's where your employer lives. Amazon DSP is the fastest-growing skill for comp, especially at retail-heavy brands and agencies.

Here's how to choose.

## Trade Desk: the default answer for most programmatic careers

The Trade Desk is the best DSP to learn if you're starting out or switching into programmatic. It's the most widely adopted independent DSP at U.S. agencies—Horizon, Tinuiti, Publicis, Havas, and most mid-sized independents run campaigns through TTD.

You'll find Trade Desk seats at holding-company agencies (especially Publicis and IPG), independent programmatic shops, and larger brands running in-house programmatic teams. The UI is intuitive enough to learn in weeks but deep enough that senior traders spend years mastering bid strategies, audience layering, and creative optimization.

From a hiring perspective, "2+ years Trade Desk experience" appears in 60-70% of programmatic job postings in 2026. It's the baseline fluency employers assume you have. If you only learn one DSP, make it this one.

Salary-wise, mid-level programmatic traders with Trade Desk experience are running $70K-$90K at agencies, $80K-$100K in-house at brands. Senior programmatic managers with TTD fluency hit $95K-$115K. Not the highest ceiling, but the widest hiring pool.

The downside: Trade Desk doesn't integrate with Google Analytics 4, Google Campaign Manager 360, or YouTube in the seamless way DV360 does. If your agency or brand is deeply embedded in Google Marketing Platform, you'll hit friction.

## DV360: essential if you're in the Google ecosystem

DV360 is the right DSP to learn if you're at an agency or brand that runs Google Marketing Platform. WPP's GroupM, Omnicom's OMG, and Dentsu all have GMP partnerships. If your team is running campaigns in CM360, measuring in GA4, and buying YouTube direct, DV360 is the programmatic hub.

The platform has a steeper learning curve than Trade Desk—more nested menus, more reliance on Google's taxonomy, more dependencies on Campaign Manager for tracking. But if you're already working in the Google stack, the integration payoff is significant. Cross-channel attribution, YouTube reservations, and Google's first-party audience signals all flow more cleanly through DV360 than through an independent DSP.

From a comp perspective, DV360 skills command similar salaries to Trade Desk at mid-levels ($70K-$90K agency, $80K-$100K in-house), but senior roles requiring both DV360 and the broader GMP stack push higher—$100K-$125K for programmatic leads at agencies, $115K-$140K for senior programmatic managers at brands.

The trap: DV360 fluency doesn't transfer as cleanly to non-Google environments. If you leave a GMP-centric agency for an independent shop running Trade Desk and Amazon DSP, your DV360 expertise loses value fast. It's a higher-risk specialization than Trade Desk.

## Amazon DSP: the fastest comp growth, narrower role fit

Amazon DSP is the best DSP to learn if you're targeting retail media roles or brands with heavy Amazon retail partnerships. Comp growth for Amazon DSP skills is outpacing Trade Desk and DV360 in 2026—senior programmatic managers with Amazon DSP fluency are seeing offers 12-18% above peers with only Trade Desk experience.

Retail media is where the programmatic budget growth is. Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect, Kroger Precision Marketing, Instacart Ads—brands are shifting spend into closed-loop environments where they can tie media to SKU-level sales. Amazon DSP is the largest and most mature of these platforms, and agencies are scrambling to staff up.

If you're at a CPG brand, a retail-focused agency, or pursuing an in-house role at a company selling on Amazon, Amazon DSP fluency can bump your offer by $10K-$15K. Mid-level programmatic specialists with Amazon DSP experience are running $75K-$95K at agencies, $85K-$105K in-house. Senior programmatic managers with deep Amazon DSP and retail media expertise are hitting $105K-$130K.

The limitation: Amazon DSP is a narrower platform than Trade Desk or DV360. It's optimized for Amazon's retail and streaming inventory (Freevee, Prime Video, Twitch, Thursday Night Football) and off-Amazon third-party supply that integrates with Amazon's demand-side signals. If you're not working on retail-heavy accounts, you won't touch it. And if you're building a generalist programmatic career, Amazon DSP alone won't open as many doors as Trade Desk.

## Which DSP to learn: the decision tree

Start with Trade Desk if you're early in your programmatic career, working at an agency or brand without deep Google or Amazon partnerships, or trying to maximize hiring optionality. It's the most portable skill.

Learn DV360 first (or second) if you're at an agency with Google Marketing Platform seats—WPP, Omnicom, Dentsu—or a brand running GA4, CM360, and YouTube as the core of your marketing stack. The integration benefits justify the steeper learning curve.

Add Amazon DSP if you're at a retail-focused agency, a CPG brand, or pursuing roles where Amazon retail media is a significant budget line. It's the highest comp growth area in programmatic right now, but the role fit is narrower.

Don't try to learn all three at once. Employers value depth over superficial familiarity. Spend 6-12 months becoming proficient in one platform—running live campaigns, troubleshooting delivery issues, optimizing toward KPIs—before adding a second DSP to your skill set.

## The trade desk vs DV360 decision for 2026

If you're choosing between Trade Desk and DV360, the answer comes down to where you work and where you want to work next.

Trade Desk is the better default. It's taught at more agencies, appears in more job postings, and transfers more cleanly across holding companies, independents, and in-house teams. If you're at Horizon, Tinuiti, Basis Technologies, or most mid-sized agencies, Trade Desk is the platform you'll use daily.

DV360 is the better choice if your current or target employer is locked into Google Marketing Platform. GroupM's Finecast (their CTV offering) runs on DV360. Omnicom's Omni platform integrates with DV360. If you're at one of these agencies or targeting them, DV360 fluency is non-negotiable.

The comp difference between Trade Desk and DV360 skills is minimal at junior and mid-levels. At senior levels, DV360 + broader GMP fluency (CM360, GA4, Google Ads) pushes higher than Trade Desk alone, but that's the full-stack premium, not the DSP itself.

## Amazon DSP vs Trade Desk: the retail media calculation

Amazon DSP vs Trade Desk isn't an either-or for most careers—it's a sequencing question. Trade Desk first for breadth, Amazon DSP second if you're moving into retail media.

Amazon DSP roles are growing faster than Trade Desk roles in 2026. Brands are hiring programmatic managers specifically to own Amazon DSP, Walmart Connect, and Instacart. Agencies are building dedicated retail media practices. The comp premiums reflect that demand.

But Amazon DSP without Trade Desk leaves you pigeonholed. If the retail media wave plateaus or your brand cuts Amazon spend, your transferable skills are limited. Trade Desk + Amazon DSP is the stronger combination for long-term career mobility.

If you're already in a retail-heavy role—managing programmatic for a CPG brand, working at an agency with Walmart or Amazon as anchor clients—learn Amazon DSP immediately. The comp upside justifies front-loading that skill. Otherwise, Trade Desk first.

## What employers actually care about in 2026

Employers hiring for [programmatic](/jobs/function/programmatic/) roles in 2026 care less about which DSP you know and more about whether you can run full-funnel campaigns, troubleshoot delivery issues, and optimize toward business outcomes.

That said, the DSP on your resume determines which interviews you get. Trade Desk gets you the most first-round interviews. DV360 gets you into Google-centric agencies and brands. Amazon DSP gets you into retail media roles with the highest comp growth.

The best programmatic careers in 2026 require fluency in at least two DSPs. Start with the one that matches where you are now, add the second that aligns with where you want to be in two years.
